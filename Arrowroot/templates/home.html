Supports Digestive Health

Arrowroot is gentle on the stomach and aids in digestion, making it beneficial for individuals with sensitive digestive systems or recovering from illness.

Gluten-Free Alternative

As a naturally gluten-free starch, arrowroot serves as a safe and versatile thickening agent for people with celiac disease or gluten intolerance.

Nutrient-Rich

Arrowroot contains essential nutrients such as potassium, iron, and B vitamins, which contribute to healthy energy levels, improved circulation, and overall well-being.

Arrowroot Growth Journey

Day 1 Week 2 1 Month 2-4 Months 5-7 Months 8-12 Months

Seed/Tuber Stage (Day 1 – Week 2)

• Planting starts with rhizomes (not true seeds).

• Sprouts begin to emerge after a few days to 2 weeks.

Arrowroot begins from a planted rhizome, sprouting into seedlings within two weeks. The plant grows steadily, producing tall leafy stems and roots over the next few months. By 5–7 months, tubers start swelling underground while leaves may flower above. After 8–10 months, the plant reaches maturity with fully formed tubers. At 10–12 months, when the leaves begin to dry, arrowroot is ready for harvest.
